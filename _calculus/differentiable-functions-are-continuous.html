Differentiable Functions are Continuous

Let $A\subseteq\mathbb{R}^n$, $\vec{a}\in \text{int}(A)$, and $f:A\to\mathbb{R}^m$. If $f$ is differentiable at $\vec{A}$, then it is continuous at $\vec{a}$.
